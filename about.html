A small app, made by one person.

Connect is built by Mahdi, in Paris. No team, no roadmap deck, no funding round. One person, picking the next thing carefully.

The app started as a fresh rebuild of an earlier prototype. The clean codebase, the editorial voice, and the offline hub were all carried over from that effort.

01.

Why it exists.

Most social apps are built around what makes their numbers go up. Connect is built around what someone would actually want to come back to.

A small refusal

To do social differently.

The mainstream social apps are very good at one thing: holding attention as long as possible. They got there by removing friction, blurring time, and treating every interaction as a metric. The product of that is a feed that doesn't end, and a vague feeling that it cost something.

Connect refuses some of those moves on purpose. Stories fade. Refresh reorders. The feed surfaces interest-matched questions, not whatever is loudest. Tide gives back a moment of attention instead of taking one.

A long bet

That fewer, better surfaces win.

The bet is that a social app that respects time, makes its boundaries explicit, and gives you something useful when it can't connect, is the kind of thing you keep installed for years instead of months.

02.

How it's built.

A Flutter app on iOS and Android. Supabase for auth, storage, database, and realtime. Hosted in the EU.

ClientCross-platform

Flutter.

A single codebase ships to iOS and Android. Custom paint for Tide, Net, Spin, and Faker. WebRTC for live audio. Google Fonts on the editorial surfaces.

BackendManaged

Supabase.

Auth, Postgres, storage, realtime. Row-level security gates every read and write. Hosted in the EU on European servers.

AITagging

Groq.

A Groq agent on llama-3.3-70b writes the debate tags. A separate post agent powers the adaptive quizzes. Both use strict sanitisers on output.

AdsFree tier

AdMob + in-house.

Native, interstitial, and app-open formats through google_mobile_ads. A small Supabase-backed in-house system covers community causes.
