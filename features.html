01.

Stories that fade.

A moment, kept for twenty-four hours, then gone. Replies tie back to the story so the conversation never drifts.

Lifecycle

Twenty-four hours. Then nothing.

Each story has a hard expiry. There's a small grace period if someone reposts you, so the thread can finish making sense. Past the window, a chat reply that referenced the story says "Story not available."

No archive pressure. No dashboards to feed.

02.

Debates with a tag agent.

A small reasoning agent reads each post and writes three to five taxonomy slugs onto it. A sibling-topic graph fans the post out to adjacent interests.

Behind the feed

The room finds the question.

No hashtags to invent, no algorithm to game. The tagger uses the same taxonomy that powers your home interests. If your interest in "music" is close to "live performance," debates on either find you without spam.

Cast a side, see how the room leans, jump into the thread. Or just watch.

Built so good takes don't need to shout.

03.

Tide. A resonance pacer.

Five seconds in, five seconds out. Six breaths per minute. The protocol with the strongest acute non-pharmacologic effect on heart-rate variability known.

Clinical, not gimmicky

The body settles to a rhythm.

The outer ring breathes on the protocol. Your warm disc follows your touch. The closer your edge tracks the pacer ring, the higher your coherence climbs and the more the whole canvas glows. A soft haptic on each phase transition lets you keep rhythm with your eyes closed.

04.

Feed that respects your time.

Refresh reorders posts you've already seen instead of hiding them. The Refine card slot moves around so you actually notice it. Nothing pretends to be urgent.

Sane defaults

Refresh, don't bait.

Hide-on-seen is the cheap trick that makes feeds feel infinite. Connect's refresh shuffles instead. The Refine card, the surface where you tune your interests, slides into a different slot on every refresh so you bump into it again.

Interest-matched debates land in the same feed as people you follow. The mix is yours to tune.

05.

Quizzes built from posts.

Channel shorts and shared links can spin up a short adaptive quiz. Three to five questions, no padding, no trick questions.

Adaptive · short · honest

A quick check on what stuck.

The shared quiz engine sanitises each question with a strict per-question pass, and the analysis prompt is tightened with a disambiguation block so the questions don't drift onto things the source didn't actually say.

Verified working on June 1, 2026.

Made for retention, not for streaks.
