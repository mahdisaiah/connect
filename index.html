01.

Stories that fade.

Twenty-four hours. Replies thread back to the moment they were sent about. Then it's gone, on purpose.

A moment, returned to.

Stays just long enough to matter.

Post a thought, a photo, a voice note. Friends reply inside the story so the conversation doesn't drift. If someone reposts you, the original sticks around long enough for the thread to make sense. After twenty-four hours it disappears.

Past that window, a chat reply says, plainly, "Story not available." No traces, no archive pressure.

Made to be ephemeral. So you can be honest.

02.

Debates that matter.

A small AI agent reads each debate and tags it with three to five taxonomy slugs. Then the feed surfaces it to the people who actually care.

Quick takes, on real questions.

Not what's loud. What you care about.

Post a question. The tagger writes a few topic slugs onto it from the same taxonomy that drives your home feed. Sibling topics fan out so adjacent interests find their way in. No hashtags to invent, no algorithm to game.

Cast a side, see how the room leans, jump into the thread. Or just watch.

The room is who's listening. The feed makes sure they hear.

03.

Tide. Breathe with a stranger.

A clinical-grade resonance pacer built into the app. Five seconds in, five seconds out, six breaths per minute. Try it here.

Hold to inhale · release to exhale

The pacer ring breathes on the protocol. Your warm disc follows your touch. The closer you track it, the more the screen glows.

Protocol
Resonant breathing at 6 BPM.
In / Out
Five seconds inhale, five seconds exhale. No hold phase.
Why it works
Maximises baroreflex gain. Lifts vagal tone. Shifts autonomic balance toward parasympathetic.
Cited
Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014. Steffen et al., 2017. Elliott, 2008.
Hardware
Soft haptic on each phase transition so you can keep rhythm with your eyes closed.
04.

When the network drops, a small room opens.

Four games. No accounts, no servers, no scores beyond what's on the screen. They run when the rest of the app can't.

i.One player

Net.

Rotate to mend the network. The grid stitches itself shut as you go.

ii.One player

Tide.

Breathe with a stranger. The resonance pacer, lifted out of the app and given its own room.

iii.Two to ten

Spin.

A wheel decides who must answer. Truth or dare, with a casino wheel that ratchets under your thumb.

iv.Four to ten

Faker.

One of you knows nothing. The phone passes around. Two lines each. Then a vote, then a guess.
