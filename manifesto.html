The why Read in two minutes

The shorter the better.

Most social apps measure how long you stayed.
Connect measures whether you wanted to stay at all.

It is built on a few small refusals.

  1. i.

    No infinite scroll bait.

    Refresh reorders, it doesn't hide. Seen posts stay visible. The Refine card moves so you remember it's there.

  2. ii.

    No archive pressure.

    Stories disappear after twenty-four hours. Replies that reference them say so, plainly.

  3. iii.

    No selling your data.

    Not to advertisers, not to brokers, not for "research." If it ever changes, it'll be announced in the app before it takes effect.

  4. iv.

    No dark patterns.

    Cancel is one tap. Notifications are off by default. There are no streaks to break, no flames to lose.

  5. v.

    No noisy AI copy.

    User-visible text stays plain. Conversational. Written by a person, edited by a person.

The goal isn't time spent.
It's whether you feel better
when you close the app
than when you opened it.

And a few small commitments.

  • Ship features when they're ready, not on a calendar.
  • Prune surfaces that aren't earning their keep.
  • Keep Tide as it is. No badges, no streaks, no leaderboards.
  • Keep the offline hub working on-device, forever.
  • Tell you what changed, in the changelog, in plain words.

Connect is a small social app.
Made on purpose.
